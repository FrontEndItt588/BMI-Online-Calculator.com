What is

BMI?

Body mass index (BMI) is a measurement of a person's weight with respect to his or her height. It is more of an indicator than a direct measurement of a person's total body fat

BMI Table

BMI is a measure of weight in relation to height. It is the most practical way to estimate if you are underweight, normal weight, overweight, moderately obese, severely obese or very severely obese. The figure below shows which category your BMI falls into.



Underweight

BMI Range: 18.4 and below
Health Risk: Malnutrition risk

Normal Weight

BMI Range: 18.5-24.9
Health Risk: Low risk

Overweight

BMI Range: 25-29.9
Health Risk: Enchanced risk

Moderately Obese

BMI Range: 30-34.9
Health Risk: Medium risk

Severely Obese

BMI Range: 35-39.9
Health Risk: Malnutrition risk

Very Severely Obese

BMI Range: 40 and above
Health Risk: Very high risk
